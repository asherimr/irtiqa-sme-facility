The Strategic Objective

To engineer an asset-backed ecosystem that transitions unbanked SMEs from short-term liquidity (via Trade-Based Salam) to long-term growth (via Equity-Based Mudarabah) using a mechanism that eliminates bank risk exposure.

Collateral Deadlock

Banks have liquidity, but SMEs lack real estate. Result: SME financing is <7% of private credit.

Moral Hazard

"Information Asymmetry" creates fear of under-reported profits or fund diversion in Mudarabah.

Ops Inefficiency

Processing small loans costs the same as corporate loans, making traditional models unviable.

Price Risk

In standard Salam, banks own the goods. If prices crash, the bank takes a loss.

The Solution

The Credit Ladder

Replacing "Trust" with "Structure."

Tier 1: Secured Liquidity

Parallel Salam (Working Capital)

Designed for 30-90 day cycles. The bank funds production but never holds the bag.

1
  • Anchor Lock: Price locked via "Wa'ad" (Promise to Purchase) from Brand/Exporter before funding.
  • Closed Loop: Bank pays Supplier directly. SME gets yarn, not cash.

The Innovation

10-15% of SME Profit per cycle is frozen.

The "Hamish Jiddiyah" Accumulator

From every trade cycle, the Bank retains profit in a frozen Security Deposit. Over 6-12 months, this builds Cash Collateral.

  • Bank Role: Wakeel (Agent). No Capital Risk.
  • Safety Net: Secured against accumulated Hamish Jiddiyah.
2

Tier 2: Growth Engine

Restricted Mudarabah (Expansion)

Unlocked after 10 cycles. Bank connects SME to HNW investors for expansion capital (Machinery/Scaling).
